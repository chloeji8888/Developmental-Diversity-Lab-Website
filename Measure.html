CHEERS

Description: Children’s Household-Environment Engagement Rating Scale: Based on the POPE, the CHEERS assesses a child’s level of engagement in the home. Trained video coders rated the child’s engagement level during two-minute intervals on five possible levels of engagement. The levels of engagement were inspired by levels of engagement on the POPE, examining previous research on children’s activities at home, and consulting with research faculty. The possible levels of engagement on the CHEERS are: (a) Isolated (b) Non-engaged. (c) Interactive non-reciprocal. (d) Reciprocal.
Purpose: CHEERS was designed for Independent Evaluators to code child engagement in parent-recorded home videos pre- and post-intervention.
Development: Developed by Dr. Jeffrey Wood and Dr. John Danial.

ARCHER (Previously SOARS)

Description: Autism-Related Characteristics Home Evaluation Rating (ARCHER) Scale. The ARCHER scale was developed as a measure of common Autism-related behaviors (ARBs) in the home environment. Unlike clinic-based assessments, ARCHER entails observation without standardized prompts and activities. These items were selected based on a review of the autism literature and commonly used measures of ARBs.
Purpose: ARCHER was designed for Independent Evaluators to code child autism-related behaviors in parent-recorded home videos pre-and post-intervention.
Development: Development of the measure began with selection of relevant observable ARBs (see Danial, 2015; the measure was originally entitled SOARS).

Empathy Processes in Childhood and Adolescence

Description: The YTP symptom categories reflect a wide range of behavioral and symptomatic patterns associated with autism, as reported by caregivers.
Development: The YTP Symptom categories are 43 groups of problems derived from a full set of 287 caregiver-reported problems through inductive data reduction methods.
Examples: The YTP symptom categories encompass social communication (SC), restricted and repetitive behaviors (RRB)s, externalizing and internalizing challenges.

YTP interview

Description: A one-hour interview for parents of children with autism that attempts to comprehensively identify personalized symptoms and treatment goals across most mental health settings while accounting for the heterogeneity of autism and functional domains.
Purpose: The interview generates a symptom list of up to 12 per child, all in the parents' own words, and can be used to monitor short-term improvements in treatment and indicate when to switch clinical focus by attaining weekly ratings from the parents.

Empathy Videos

Description: Video vignettes presented to children with autism that measured empathy through five distinct emotions: 1) sadness, 2) fear, 3) embarrassment, 4) anger, and 5) happiness. Children identified the emotion that the protagonist felt in each vignette, as well as described a time when they also felt that emotion. They also rated the strength (i.e., valence) of their feelings using a Likert scale.
Purpose: Throughout the presentation of video vignettes, children’s verbal responses, facial affect, and eye gaze were also coded to measure components of empathy such as prosocial motivation and perspective taking.

The Modular EBPs for Youth with Autism Fidelity Scale (MEYA-FS; McLeod et al., 2022)

Description: Addresses variation in adherence and competence of core practices within modular EBPs for youth with autism
Purpose: MEYA-FS was developed to document practitioner change in the use of and expertise in core practices in SEBASTIEN, BIACA, and similar EBP packages for autistic youth throughout training and clinical utilization.
Development: McLeod et al., 2022
